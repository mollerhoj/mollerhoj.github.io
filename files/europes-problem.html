Europe Has a Problem: It's Norway!

Norway’s Oil Wealth and Europe’s Resource Imbalance

The European continent is rich in natural resources, yet, unlike America, China, or Russia, this wealth is not being used to support a substantial army. Instead, Norway hoards its oil wealth in the world’s largest sovereign wealth fund—the Norwegian Oil Fund—leaving Europe in a vulnerable position. To better secure its own future and that of Europe, Norway should revise the fiscal rules governing the fund to allow for a retroactive withdrawal of 3% since the start of the Ukraine war. This would be more than enough to win the war against Putin, despite Trump's treachery.

Norway, a country of just over five million people, sits on a trove of oil and gas reserves in the North Sea. The revenues from this resource bonanza are truly unfathomable, currently exceeding 1,690 billion euros, up from 1,170 since the war started. Meanwhile, the U.S. and Europe have contributed only 246 billion euros in aid to Ukraine. Norway could have contributed double that amount and still come out ahead!

So, how much has Norway spent so far? A mere 4 billion euros on aid, adding to the 3 billion already given, totaling less than 8 billion. To put this in perspective, Norway is still behind Denmark, a nation of similar population size that relies primarily on its innovative industries.

The American and Russian Models: Oil as a Strategic Asset

The United States, the world’s largest oil producer, channels much of its wealth into maintaining a powerful military, with spending exceeding $800 billion annually. Similarly, Russia uses its oil and gas revenues to fund military ambitions, with state-owned giants like Rosneft and Gazprom supporting its military-industrial complex. In contrast, Norway invests its wealth in global markets, doing little to enhance Europe’s military standing.

The Risk to Norway’s Own Security

If Russia succeeds in its invasion of Ukraine, Norway’s natural resources could be at risk. The belief that Norway can remain untouched while accumulating wealth is naive. A world where military power dictates national boundaries puts Norway’s oil fields in jeopardy. Without a strong military and strategic investments in European security, Norway itself could be vulnerable.

Europe’s Dilemma: A Rich Ally Without Strategic Commitment

Europe’s major powers face a paradox: they are aligned with one of the world’s wealthiest nations per capita, yet Norway’s contributions to collective security are modest. Despite its vast oil wealth, Norway’s defense spending hovers around 2% of GDP, just meeting NATO’s minimum threshold. In contrast, countries like Poland and the Baltic states invest significantly in their military capabilities despite their smaller resources. If Norway committed even a fraction of its oil wealth to European security, it could dramatically transform the continent’s defense posture.

A Call for Strategic Responsibility

The Norwegian Oil Fund aims to secure the wealth of future generations of Norwegians, but what good is this wealth if Norway’s security is compromised? By increasing military spending and supporting Ukraine, Norway would not only bolster European security but also protect its long-term interests. If Europe is to stand strong in an era of great power competition, Norway must step up—not just as an economic powerhouse, but as a strategic ally willing to share the load.
